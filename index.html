Unit 1 ‚Äî Data Collection & Sampling Methods

Core: survey steps, primary vs secondary data, census vs sample, sampling methods (random & non-random).

Sampling methods ‚Äî quick reference

  • Simple Random Sampling ‚Äî equal chance, use random numbers.
  • Stratified Sampling ‚Äî divide into strata, sample in each; reduces variance.
  • Cluster Sampling ‚Äî sample clusters (villages/blocks), then units inside.
  • Systematic Sampling ‚Äî every k-th unit from a listing.
  • Non-Probability Methods ‚Äî Purposive, Convenience, Quota (used when frame unavailable).

How examiners ask (5M / 15M)

5M: Define & one example. 15M: Classification + advantages/limitations + short example (NSSO, household consumption survey).

Unit 2 ‚Äî Survey Tools & Techniques

Questionnaires, schedules, types of questions, pilot studies, and qualitative methods.

Questionnaire design ‚Äî essentials

  • Simple, neutral wording ‚Äî avoid leading or double-barrel questions.
  • Logical order: screening ‚Üí demographics ‚Üí main questions ‚Üí closing.
  • Use mix of open & closed questions; include filter questions.
  • Pilot test with a small sample; refine wording and flow.

Qualitative tools ‚Äî short notes

  • Observation: Direct recording of events/behaviour.
  • Focus Groups: Moderator-led group discussion for opinions.
  • Delphi: Iterative expert consultation to build consensus.
  • Depth Interviews: One-to-one, in-depth exploratory interviews.

Unit 3 ‚Äî Participatory Research (PRA)

Community-centred tools: social map, resource map, transect walk, timelines ‚Äî used for local context and community planning.

PRA tools ‚Äî practice to draw

  • Social Map: Sketch households, roads, facilities.
  • Resource Map: Mark land, water, grazing, forests.
  • Transect Walk: Walk along a transect to observe land use & resources.
  • Timeline: Community events (droughts, floods, interventions).

Unit 4 ‚Äî Attitude Measurement & Scaling

Measurement scales & scaling techniques for attitudes: NOIR & Likert, Thurstone, Guttman.

NOIR ‚Äî remember the scale types

  • Nominal: Categories (e.g., religion)
  • Ordinal: Ordered ranks (e.g., satisfaction level)
  • Interval: Equal intervals (e.g., temperature Celsius)
  • Ratio: True zero (e.g., income)

Sample Likert item: "I find local healthcare services accessible."

Model Answers ‚Äî Quick & Full

5-mark answers (memorize these)

  • Pilot Study: A small trial survey conducted to test the questionnaire, field procedures and logistics before the main survey; helps identify errors and estimate time and cost.
  • Likert Scale: A rating scale measuring degree of agreement on ordered categories (e.g., 1‚Äì5). Score items and sum to get an attitude index.
  • Simple Random Sampling: A method in which every unit in the population has an equal chance of selection, usually achieved using random numbers.

15-mark model (Questionnaire Design)

Definition: A questionnaire is a structured instrument of questions designed to collect information from respondents for a survey.

Principles: clarity, neutrality, single idea per question, logical flow and use of filters.

Pilot Testing: Small trial with 20‚Äì50 respondents; revise wording and skip patterns based on feedback.

Practical tips: Keep demographics short, avoid ambiguous scales, train interviewers and include consent statement.

Conclusion: A well-designed questionnaire ensures data validity and reduces non-sampling errors.

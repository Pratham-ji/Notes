Unit I — Introduction to Indian Knowledge System

  • IKS refers to indigenous, traditional, and classical knowledge systems developed in India.
  • Rooted in Dharma, ethics, sustainability, harmony with nature.
  • Transmitted through Guru–Shishya Parampara and oral traditions.
  • Holistic approach: mind, body, society, environment.
  • IKS includes science, medicine, philosophy, governance, ecology, arts.
  • Modern relevance: Yoga, Ayurveda, sustainability, ethics.
  • Promoted officially under NEP-2020.

Unit II — Indian Scholars, Literature & Sciences

Philosophy & Literature

  • Vyasa — Mahabharata, Vedanta
  • Manu — Manusmriti (law)
  • Kanad — Vaisheshika, atomism
  • Panini — Sanskrit grammar (Ashtadhyayi)
  • Nagarjuna — Buddhist philosophy
  • Pingala — Binary numbers, Chandas

Mathematics & Astronomy

  • Aryabhata — π, Earth rotation
  • Brahmagupta — Zero, negative numbers
  • Bhaskaracharya — Early calculus
  • Bodhayana — Pythagoras theorem
  • Varahamihira — Astronomy

Medicine & Yoga

  • Charaka — Ayurveda
  • Susruta — Surgery
  • Dhanvantari — God of Ayurveda
  • Patanjali — Yoga Sutras

Unit III — Tribal, Traditional & Ethnic Communities

  • Traditional ecological knowledge of forests, soil, water.
  • Sustainable resource use and seasonal harvesting.
  • Livelihoods linked with rituals and culture.
  • Tangible heritage: tools, crafts, monuments.
  • Intangible heritage: songs, folklore, rituals.

Unit IV — Traditional Practices & Applied Knowledge

  • Myths, rituals, taboos, folk stories.
  • Sacred groves — biodiversity protection.
  • Traditional agriculture and water conservation.
  • Handicrafts, dyes, pigments, metallurgy.
  • Vaidya — Ayurveda practitioner.
  • Amchi — Himalayan medicine system.

Unit V — Protection, Preservation & Conservation of IKS

  • Documentation of oral and written traditions.
  • Digitization and archival preservation.
  • Community-based conservation.
  • TKDL — Traditional Knowledge Digital Library.
  • Prevention of biopiracy.

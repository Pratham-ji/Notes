Unit I – Introduction to Poetry

What is Poetry?

Poetry is a literary form that expresses emotions, thoughts, and experiences through condensed language, rhythm, imagery, and symbolic meaning. Unlike prose, poetry relies on musicality and layered interpretation.

Types of Poetry

  • Epic: Long narrative poem (Homer’s Iliad)
  • Ballad: Narrative poem in simple language
  • Ode: Lyric poem expressing admiration
  • Sonnet: 14-line poem (Shakespearean / Petrarchan)
  • Elegy: Poem of mourning (Gray)
  • Lyric: Short personal poem

Poetic Devices

  • Metaphor, Simile, Personification
  • Alliteration, Assonance
  • Irony and Satire
  • Rhyme & Rhythm
  • Blank Verse (unrhymed iambic pentameter)

Unit II – Classical & Metaphysical Poetry

Homer – The Iliad

An epic poem centered on the Trojan War. Themes include heroism, fate, honor, and human suffering.

Shakespeare – Sonnets & Songs

  • Blow, Blow Thou Winter Wind: Critique of human ingratitude
  • Sonnet 30: Memory, loss, and emotional endurance

John Donne – A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning

A metaphysical poem using intellectual conceits (compass imagery) to portray spiritual love.

John Milton – When the Assault Was Intended to the City

Reflects Puritan faith, divine protection, and political anxiety.

“Some poems don’t demand answers — only honesty.”

Unit III – Neo-Classical Poetry

Alexander Pope

  • An Essay on Man: Reason, order, human limitation
  • Ode on Solitude: Simple life ideal

Thomas Gray

  • Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard: Mortality, common man
  • The Epitaph: Humility and spiritual peace
Exam Tip: Gray’s Elegy is a frequent long-answer question.
“Even silence has meaning when you learn how to read it.”

Section A – Exact Answers (Memorize)

1. Poetry is a literary form that expresses emotions and ideas through rhythm, imagery, and symbolic language. It differs from prose in its musical structure and condensed expression. 2. A sonnet is a fourteen-line poem written in iambic pentameter, commonly associated with themes of love, time, and beauty. 3. An elegy is a poem of mourning that reflects on death, loss, and the passage of time, often moving towards consolation. 4. Metaphysical poetry is characterized by intellectual imagery, philosophical themes, and elaborate conceits, as seen in the works of John Donne. 5. Romantic poetry emphasizes emotion, imagination, nature, and individual experience, reacting against neoclassical restraint.
